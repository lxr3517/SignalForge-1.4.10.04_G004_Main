SignalForge

Quick instruction sheet for forecasts, diagnostics, planning, and decision support

What SignalForge is for

SignalForge helps teams turn marketing, lead, cost, and revenue data into actionable forecasting, planning, and diagnostic insights. Use it to understand what is happening, what is likely to happen next, and where to adjust spend or strategy.

Best use cases

  • Forecast revenue, leads, and ROAS
  • Spot anomalies and performance shifts early
  • Understand lag between leads, spend, and revenue
  • Compare platforms, affiliates, cohorts, and risk

How to use SignalForge

1

Load clean source data

Upload or connect the required data sources such as revenue, leads, cost, platform, affiliate, and any cohort or profile-level inputs your project uses.

2

Choose the scope

Select whether you want company-wide results or a more focused analysis by platform, affiliate, cohort, or other available grouping.

3

Set your date range and training window

Use a time period that reflects the business question. Short windows react faster, while longer windows usually provide more stability.

4

Run the model

Launch the forecast or analysis run and allow SignalForge to score current conditions, detect patterns, and prepare the result views.

5

Review the output panels

Focus first on the headline metrics, forecast direction, risk level, anomalies, and any driver summaries that explain what moved the numbers.

6

Turn insight into action

Use planning, simulation, rankings, and diagnostics to decide whether to scale, reduce, monitor, or test specific traffic sources or strategies.

What to look at first

  • Forecast direction and expected range
  • Current risk or instability signals
  • Anomalies versus expected performance
  • Top positive and negative drivers
  • Platform or affiliate changes worth action

Quick interpretation guide

Forecast up: conditions are supportive, but confirm confidence and driver quality.

Forecast down: check lead mix, lag effects, anomalies, and weak sources before reacting.

High risk: treat outcomes as less stable and use scenario planning before major budget moves.

Best practice: Use SignalForge as a decision-support system, not a blind autopilot. Always compare model output with current business context.
Warning: Poor source data, missing spend, or inconsistent dates can reduce forecast quality. Verify inputs before trusting the output.
